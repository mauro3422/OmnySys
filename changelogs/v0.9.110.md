# Release v0.9.110 - Semantic Chests Architecture

**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Scope:** Semantic analysis refinement and reporting fidelity.

## Executive Summary

This release introduces the **Semantic Chests** architecture, a qualitative leap in how OmnySys understands and categorizes code. By functionalizing conceptual duplicates into specialized "chests" (`lifecycle`, `telemetry`, `storage`, `orchestration`, `logic`), the system can now distinguish between common architectural patterns (which are healthy) and genuine technical debt (which requires action).

## Major Changes

### 1. V4 Semantic Fingerprinting
- **New Format:** `verb:chest:domain:entity`.
- **Global Re-indexing:** 2,219 files and 19,901 atoms have been re-analyzed to incorporate functional categorization.
- **Improved Extraction:** Enhanced `semantic-analyzer.js` to detect infrastructure keywords (constructors, callbacks, stats, logging) and assign them to the correct functional chest.

### 2. Intelligent Severity Policy
- **Low Risk Automation:** Atoms in the `lifecycle` and `telemetry` chests are now automatically classified as **LOW** severity if they appear as conceptual duplicates.
- **High Risk Targeting:** Duplicates found in `logic` and `orchestration` are prioritized with **HIGH/MEDIUM** severity, ensuring focus remains on business-critical redundancy.

### 3. Reporting Fidelity
- **Unified Technical Debt Report:** Refined `technical-debt-report.js` to parse and display V4 fingerprints and chest-based grouping.
- **Remediation Plan Builder:** Standardized metadata propagation so canonical instances and group sizes are correctly displayed in remediation guidance.

## Metrics & Impact
- **Noise Reduction:** Successfully filtered ~540 instances of boilerplate code (constructors, initializers) from the high-priority debt backlog.
- **Improved Accuracy:** Tech Debt Score now accurately reflects the state of business logic redundancy rather than architectural noise.
- **Current Health Score:** 70/100 (Grade B) — Up from 46/100 (Grade F) due to improved categorization of "expected" duplicates.
